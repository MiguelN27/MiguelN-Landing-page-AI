## ABOUT THE COMPANY

## Brand Identity

- Name: Ashborne Guitars
- Tagline: "Born from the grain"
- Founded: 1987 — Brevard, North Carolina
- Founder: Miguel Narvaez (third-generation furniture maker)
- Current CEO/Creative Director: June Ashborne (Eli's daughter, Berklee graduate)
- Team: 28 employees, ~900 instruments/year
- Status: Privately held, no outside investors

## Core Philosophy

The wood speaks first. Ashborne sources tonewood from sustainably managed Appalachian forests — ash, walnut, cherry, spalted maple. Every instrument showcases the natural character of the timber: grain patterns, color variation, and intentional knots are features, not flaws. No two guitars look alike.
Old-world hand luthiery meets modern playability. Every neck is hand-carved; electronics are designed in-house with working musicians. Tone is something grown, not manufactured.

## Brand Voice

Warm, grounded, confident without arrogance. Speaks like a craftsperson — specific, tactile, unhurried. Avoids corporate jargon. Favors words like: grain, hand-carved, sustainably sourced, tonal character, one-of-a-kind.

## Product Lines
 
### Hearthstone Series (Acoustic) — Flagship
 
- Dreadnought and OM body shapes
- Solid Appalachian ash or cherry tops, walnut back/sides
- Rich, balanced voice with strong midrange warmth
- **Starting at $2,800**
### Ridgeline Series (Electric)
 
- Semi-hollow and solid body configurations
- Carved ash tops, hand-wound Ashborne pickups
- Glassy clean tones; warm, articulate overdrive
- Favored by indie, blues, and alternative players
- **Starting at $3,200**
### Canopy Series (Archtop Jazz)
 
- Full-hollow archtop, spruce or cedar top, figured maple back
- Floating pickup, wooden tailpiece
- Deep, complex, classic woody jazz voice
- **Starting at $4,500**
### Understory Series (Bass)
 
- 4- and 5-string, solid and semi-hollow
- Walnut and ash bodies, maple necks
- Punchy, defined low end
- **Starting at $2,600**
### Players Series (Acoustic & Electric) — Accessible Line
 
- Same tonewoods and design DNA, streamlined production
- Acoustic and electric models available
- **$1,200–$1,800**
### Salvage Collection (Limited)
 
- One-of-a-kind instruments from reclaimed storm-felled Appalachian timber
- Provenance card with tree origin documentation
- **$3,500–$7,000+**
### Ember Series (Archive / Not in Production)
 
- 6 legendary guitars from the 1997 fire's charred workshop beams
- Only 3 accounted for today
- **Collector value: $40,000–$60,000 each**
